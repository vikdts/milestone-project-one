Diving in Koh Tao

Koh Tao is a small island of 21 km², part of the Chumphon Archipelago on the western shore of the gulf of Thailand. Along the coral-encrusted rock pinnacles, could often present a glimpse of a whale shark or eagle ray all year round, more often through April and May. Plethora of colorful reef fish is a must for young explorer.

Sunny weather provides bright waters to explore variety of rock and coral formations. Critters, nudibranch and three species of shrimp inside crevices and gaps between rocks are visible from july through september, as visibility goes down to 30 meters. Calm warm waters don't go under temperatures of 29°C and exhibit a variety of the marine life, like titan triggerfish, sea snake, hawksbill and green turtles and many others.
